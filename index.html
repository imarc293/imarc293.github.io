1920s - Flapper Era

Fashion in the 1920s reflected a major shift towards freedom and rebellion, especially for women. Influenced by jazz music, styles became more playful and less restrictive. Drop-waists, fringe, and sequins became popular trends during this time.

1950s - Classic Retro

 Fashion in the 1950s focused on elegance, structure, and a polished appearance. After World War II, styles became more defined, emphasizing traditional femininity and a "put-together" look. Full skirts, cinched waists, and polka dots became popular trends. 

1970s - Disco + Boho

Fashion in the 1970s fashion was all about self-expression and individuality. Influenced by music and cultural shifts, styles ranged from relaxed bohemian looks to bold disco outfits. Bell bottom pants, platform shoes, and bold prints were popular trends at the time.
